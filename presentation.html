❓ The Problem

⚠️ Operational Challenges

No unified system to record and track incident reports across Riyadh Metro's 41 stations across 2 lines

📋 Paper-Based Reports

Heavy reliance on paper reports and phone calls to relay information between operators and field teams

⏱️ Response Time

Delays in dispatching the right teams (ambulance, civil defense, police, maintenance) to incidents

🔍 Incident Tracking

Difficulty analyzing historical incident data and generating statistical reports

💡 The Solution

A fully integrated web application for real-time incident management

1

Unified Reporting

8-section comprehensive form covering all aspects of an incident

2

Station-Based Access

Staff see only their station's incidents — OCC Operators see all

3

Reports & Analytics

Excel / PDF / Email export with 8 detail sheets per incident

4

Enterprise Security

JWT + bcrypt + role-based access + full audit logging

🔒 Security

🛡️ Authentication

• JWT tokens with 12-hour expiry
• bcrypt password hashing (12 rounds)
• Station-based authorization

📝 Audit Log

• Every create/update/delete logged
• Who performed it and when
• Admin-only access

🌐 HTTP Protection

• Helmet (secure headers)
• Rate Limiting (200 req/15min)
• CORS restricted to domain
• HSTS

🔑 Authorization

• Each user sees only their station
• OCC Operators see all stations
• Admin-only staff management
